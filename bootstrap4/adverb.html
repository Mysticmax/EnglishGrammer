ADVERB

An adverb is a word that modifies (describes) a verb (he sings loudly), an adjective (very tall), another adverb (ended too quickly), or even a whole sentence (Fortunately, I had brought an umbrella). Adverbs often end in -ly, but some (such as fast) look exactly the same as their adjective counterparts.

Let's begin with some simple example

I will never follow your advice.
[मैं आपकी सलाह का कभी नहीं पालन करूंगा।]


The two brothers embraced each other warmly.
[दोनों भाइयों ने गर्मजोशी से एक दूसरे को गले लगाया।]

Next morning, he got up early.
[अगली सुबह, वह जल्दी उठ गया।]

The two went upstairs.
[दोनों ऊपर चले गए।]

She fell asleep fast.
[वह तेजी से सो गई।]

The old man walked away.
[बूढ़ा चला गया।]

We can stage the play together.
[हम एक साथ नाटक का मंचन कर सकते हैं।]

Defination

An adverb is a word that can modify a verb, an adjective, or another adverb.

Types of adverb

  1. Interrogative Adverbs.
  2. Relative Adverbs.
  3. Simple Adverbs.
  4. Focusing Adverbs
  5. Conjunctive Adverbs.
  6. Adverbs That Signal Attitude.
  7. Adverbs of Evaluation.

Example

My cat waits impatiently for his food
