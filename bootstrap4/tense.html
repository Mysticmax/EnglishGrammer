TENSE

Tense is a grammar term used to indicate whether a sentence (or verb) is an action in the past, the present or the future. An example of tense is a verb that indicates action is going on now, or a sentence about action taking place now. verbs come in three tenses: past, present, and future. The present tense is used to describe things that are happening right now, or things that are continuous. ... The future tense describes things that have yet to happen (e.g., later, tomorrow, next week, next year, three years from now).

Let's begin with some simple example


PRESENT TENSE

PRESENT SIMPLE

I go to school.
[मैं स्कूल जाता हूं।]

PRESENT CONTINUOUS
She is breaking a glass.
[वह एक गिलास तोड़ रही है।]
PRESENT PERFECT

I have eaten an apple.
[मैंने एक सेब खाया है।]

PRESENT PERFECT CONTINUOUS
They have been playing a cricket.
[वे क्रिकेट खेल रहे हैं।]
PAST TENSE

PAST SIMPLE

I went to the market.
[मैं बाजार गया था।]

PAST CONTINUOUS
The sun was shining everyday.
[सूरज हर दिन चमक रहा था।]
PAST PERFECT

They had played football.
[उन्होंने फुटबॉल खेला था।]

PAST PERFECT CONTINUOUS
We had been watching a movie .
[हम एक फिल्म देख रहे थे।]
FUTURE TENSE

FUTURE SIMPLE

He will help us later.
[वह बाद में हमारी मदद करेगा।]

FUTURE CONTINUOUS
She will be studying at the library tonight.
[वह आज रात पुस्तकालय में पढ़ रही होगी।]
FUTURE PERFECT

It will have stopped raining.
[बारिश बंद हो गई होगी।]

FUTURE PERFECT CONTINUOUS
I will have been knowing you for a week.
[मैं तुम्हें एक हफ्ते से जान रहा होगा।]

Defination

A verb is the action or state of being in a sentence.

Types of verb

  1. PRESENT TENSE.
  2. PAST TENSE.
  3. FUTURE TENSE.

Example

e.g: That Aeroplane was flying above the clouds.

Meaning in hindi

वह हवाई जहाज बादलों के ऊपर उड़ रहा था।
