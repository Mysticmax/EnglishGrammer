TENSE

Tense is a grammar term used to indicate whether a sentence (or verb) is an action in the past, the present or the future. An example of tense is a verb that indicates action is going on now, or a sentence about action taking place now. verbs come in three tenses: past, present, and future. The present tense is used to describe things that are happening right now, or things that are continuous. ... The future tense describes things that have yet to happen (e.g., later, tomorrow, next week, next year, three years from now).

Let's begin with some simple example


PRESENT TENSE

PRESENT SIMPLE

I go to school.
मैं स्कूल जाता हूं।]

PRESENT CONTINUOUS
She is breaking a glass.
[वह एक गिलास तोड़ रही है।]
PRESENT PERFECT

I have eaten an apple.
मैंने एक सेब खाया है।]

PRESENT PERFECT CONTINUOUS
They have been playing a cricket.
[वे क्रिकेट खेल रहे हैं।]

Defination

A verb is the action or state of being in a sentence.

Types of verb

  1. regular verb.
  2. irregular verb.
  3. linking verb.
  4. transitive verb.
  5. intransitive verb.
  6. finite verb.
  7. infinite verb.

Example

e.g: That Aeroplane was flying above the clouds.
