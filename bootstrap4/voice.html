VOICE

Voice is the term used to describe whether a verb is active or passive. In other words, when the subject of the verb is doing the action of the verb (e.g., "The dog bit the postman."), the verb is said to be in the active voice. So, the voice of a verb tells us whether the subject is acting or being acted upon.

Let's begin with some simple example

Ram ate rice at dinner.
[रात के खाने में राम ने चावल खाया।]


Beautiful giraffes roam the savannah.
[सवाना में खूबसूरत जिराफ घूमते हैं।]

Akbar changed the bike's tire.
[अकबर ने बाइक का टायर बदला।]

We are going to watch a movie tonight.
[हम आज रात एक फिल्म देखने जा रहे हैं।]

At dinner, six rice were eaten by Ram.
[रात के खाने में राम ने छह चावल खाए।]

The savannah is roamed by beautiful giraffes.
[सवाना में खूबसूरत जिराफ घूमते हैं।]

The bike's tire was changed by Akbar.
[बाइक का टायर अकबर ने बदला था।]

Defination

In grammar, the voice of a verb describes the relationship between the action (or state) that the verb expresses and the participants identified by its arguments (subject, object, etc.).

Types of voice

  1. Active voice
  2. passive voice

Example

e.g: The students' questions are always answered by the teacher.

Meaning in hindi

छात्रों के प्रश्नों का उत्तर हमेशा शिक्षक द्वारा दिया जाता है।
